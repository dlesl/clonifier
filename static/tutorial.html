Tutorial

This tutorial will guide you through a cloning project.

We will clone spoT from Escherichia coli into the expression vector pTrc99a.

To do this we will use the following primers:

Name Sequence Notes
pTrc99a_fwd gcatgcaagcttggctgttttg
pTrc99a_rev gtgtgaaattgttatccgctcacaattcc
spot_fwd CGGATAACAATTTCACACaggtcgtcgttaatcacaaagcgg overlaps with pTrc99a_rev
spot_rev AAACAGCCAAGCTTGCATGCtaatttcggtttcgggtgactttaatcac overlaps with pTrc99a_fwd

To add these primers, either use the "üóíÔ∏è Bulk edit" function or click here to add them automatically.

Now we can amplify our backbone. Load pTrc99a's sequence by using the "Standard Templates" menu, or click here.

Select the two primers, pTrc99a_fwd and pTrc99a_rev, and with the pTrc99a sequence open, click "PCR".

On the left side of the screen, you can see that each primer binds once. On the right side, you see that one PCR product was found.

Click on this product to open it.

If you would like, you can rename it by clicking the ‚úèÔ∏è next to "Name". Then click "Save as Fragment" to save it to the fragments list.

Now open the E. coli genome and amplify spoT with the other two primers, then save the resulting fragment.

You should now have two fragments in your list, and the spoT product has sequences complementary to the pTrc99a backbone at its ends. Select both products and click "Assemble".

The DNA assembly view opens. You will see that one circular product has been found. By hovering the mouse over the product, you can see how the two fragments are joined.

You can click on the product to open it.
