Basics

On your left you will find two lists which you are able to edit:

  • Primers
  • Sequences

Sequences

The sequences list works in a similar way, except you must first open a file to be able to save it to the list. Files in Genbank (.gb, .ape) format are supported. You can open files from your harddrive using File -> Open, or by dragging them into the window. If you drag files into the sequence list, they will be saved directly.

Some DNA sequences are available from the drop-down menu under "Standard Templates". The bacterial genomes are quite large, and may take a few seconds to load. During this time, the status bar in the bottom right of the screen will indicate that something is happening in the background.

PCR

To simulate PCR, open a sequence, select the primers you want to test, and click "PCR" at the bottom of the primer list.

You can adjust the parameters and click "Run" to repeat the PCR with the same primers and template.

You can click on a product to open it as a sequence, or save it directly to the sequences list.

Gibson Assembly

To simulate Gibson assembly, select the sequences you wish to assemble, at click "Assemble" at the bottom of the sequence list. Clonifier shows you possible circular and linear products. By holding the mouse over an entry, you can see a diagram of how the DNA fragments have been assembled. Clicking on an entry opens its sequence.
